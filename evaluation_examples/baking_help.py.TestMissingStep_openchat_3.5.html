I'm making pancakes for breakfast. I added a cup of flour, a teaspoon of salt, and a few tablespoons of sugar to a bowl. I stirred it together, then added a cup of milk, a beaten egg, and a few tablespoons of oil, and stirred until just mixed. Then I put 1/4 a cup on a hot frying pan, and flipped it when brown. But they're terrible! Why? List the main reason.

Output

The main reason your pancakes are terrible could be that you didn't let the batter rest before cooking. Resting the batter allows the flour to absorb the liquid, which helps to create a better texture in the pancakes. Additionally, overmixing the batter can cause the pancakes to be tough and dense. Make sure to mix the ingredients just until combined and let the batter rest for about 10-15 minutes before cooking.
