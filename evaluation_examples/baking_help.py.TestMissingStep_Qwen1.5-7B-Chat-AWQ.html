But they're terrible! Why? List the main reason.

Output

There could be several reasons why your pancakes turned out terrible. Here are some possible issues: 1. Incorrect ratio of ingredients: The recipe you provided includes flour, salt, sugar, milk, egg, and oil. However, without specific measurements for each ingredient, it's difficult to pinpoint the exact problem. Pancakes should have a balance between flour, liquid, and leavening agents. If the ratio is off, they might be too thick, dry, or not rise properly. 2. Too much flour: A cup of flour might be too much for a standard pancake recipe. It's common to use around 1 1/2 to 2 cups for a batch. Excess flour can make the pancakes tough and dense. 3. Not enough liquid: The cup of milk should be the dominant liquid in the mixture. If there's not enough liquid, the batter will be too thick and won't spread properly on the pan, resulting in tough pancakes. 4. Overmixing: Overmixing the batter can cause gluten to develop, which can make pancakes tough. Mix until the ingredients are just combined, and avoid kneading the batter. 5. Pan or heat: The pan might be too hot or not hot enough. If it's too hot, the pancakes will burn quickly; if it's not hot enough, they won't cook evenly. Adjust the heat to medium-high and make sure the pan is well-greased. 6. Lacking leavening: Baking powder or baking soda is essential for pancakes to rise. If you didn't add any leavening, they won't expand and rise properly. 7. Egg or oil: The egg and oil can add richness and flavor, but they don't directly contribute to the texture of the pancakes. If they're not properly incorporated or if the amount is off, it could affect the overall quality. To improve your pancakes, double-check the ingredient ratios, make sure the batter is the right consistency, and pay attention to the pan and heat. If possible, try again with the correct measurements and see if that makes a difference.
