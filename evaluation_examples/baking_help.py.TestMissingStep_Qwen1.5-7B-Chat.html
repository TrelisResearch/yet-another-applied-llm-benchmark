But they're terrible! Why? List the main reason.

Output

There could be several reasons why your pancakes turned out terrible. Here are the main possibilities: 1. Incorrect ratio of ingredients: The recipe you provided includes flour, salt, sugar, milk, egg, and oil. If the ratio of flour to liquid (in this case, milk) is off, the pancakes might be too thick or稠, leading to a tough texture. A common ratio for pancakes is 1:1 or 1:1.5 (flour to liquid). You might need to adjust the amount of flour to make it more liquidy. 2. Not enough liquid: If you only used 1/4 cup of milk, it might not be enough to create a smooth batter. Pancakes need a good amount of liquid to rise and spread properly. You might need to add more milk or even consider using a different liquid like buttermilk or yogurt. 3. Overmixing: Overmixing the batter can cause gluten to develop, which can make the pancakes tough. It's generally recommended to mix until the ingredients are just combined, not until the batter is smooth and shiny. 4. Pan or heat: The pan might be too hot, causing the pancakes to burn on the outside and not cook through properly. Adjust the heat to a medium or low setting, and let the pancakes cook for a few seconds before flipping. 5. Oil: While oil can add flavor and moisture, too much can make the pancakes greasy. If you used too much, it might affect the texture and absorbency of the batter. 6. Egg: If the egg is not fully beaten, it could create lumps in the batter, affecting the texture. Make sure to beat the egg well before adding it. 7. Baking powder or baking soda: If you didn't use any leavening agents (baking powder or baking soda), the pancakes might not rise properly, resulting in a flat and dense texture. To improve your pancakes, try adjusting the liquid to flour ratio, making sure the batter is not overmixed, and using the right heat for cooking. If you suspect leavening agents are missing, add a teaspoon of baking powder or baking soda to the mix. Give it another try, and you should have better results!
